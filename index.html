Education plan FAQ

To qualify for the Education plan, you must be:
• At least 13 years of age
• A student or educator in a structured classroom
Classrooms are defined as virtual or in-person educational programs. Some examples are:
• High schools, universities, post-graduate institutions, trade schools, polytechnics,
or colleges
• Online courses
• Bootcamps or workshops
• Design and coding academies
The following does not qualify for the Education plan:
• Self-directed education
• Early stage startups
• “Not-for-profit" or "non-profit" organizations whose use case is not teaching in classrooms
• Marketing departments at Educational institutions
• Educational programs affiliated with organizations whose primary purpose is
not educational

When you verify as a student or educator, you get an education status in Figma. That means you
have the Education plan and can create an unlimited number of Education teams.
• Teams under the Education plan have the same features and benefits as teams with the
Professional plan.
• Note that anyone you invite with edit access to your Education team will need to verify their
email and Education status. It is not possible to have a mix of paid and free editors in an
Education team.
• Under the Education plan, you can create as many Education teams as you want.
If you are a US high school district using Google Chromebooks, you can apply for our partnership
beta and get free access to Figma's Organizational tier. You can manage and deploy Figma
licenses to your school-managed Chromebooks from the Google Admin Console. Visit
figma.com/education/chromebooks for more information.

Figma needs to know the details of your education program or learning environment to verify
your education status.
1. Navigate to our education application
2. Confirm if you are a student or educator
3. Enter the reason you're applying for the free Figma Education plan
4. Search for your school in the What's your school's name menu
5. If your school isn't listed, select School not listed to enter details of your school:
1. The full name of the school. For example: Rhode Island School of Design (RISD)
2. The school's website (URL). For example: https://www.risd.edu/
6. Click the Submit button to complete the application process

United States
Figma offers 2 ways of accessing our tools, depending on the age of your students.
1. Teachers and students >13 years old can get started on their own by signing up at
Figma.com/education, verifying their education status, and creating an Education team. This
self-serve model is intended only for students over 13 years old. This model is NOT intended for
accessing Figma with students under 13 years old.
2. If you want to use Figma with students under 13 years old, your school must reach out to us
and sign our terms for schools. You can get this process started by filling out this form.
Internationally
Figma may only be used by students above the age of consent in their jurisdiction. We are always
looking to expand access to Figma, so if you want to use Figma with your students, please let us
know by reaching out to us at education@figma.com!

At Figma our mission is to make design accessible to everyone. We are committed to achieving
that goal in a way that safeguards and protects the privacy of our users. Figma only requires
minimal, non-sensitive personal data to use the Figma platform. The only personal data needed
is an email address, which we use to enable the account login function for our user, and a name.
Figma users can also add to their Figma account profile by uploading a profile photo or adding a
job title, but this information is completely optional and not required to use the Figma platform.
Figma also offers several (optional) forms of multifactor authentication, one version of which, if
chosen, requires the user to input their phone number for SMS identity verification. No sensitive
personal information is permitted on the Figma platform. School administrators can become
administrators of their school's Figma instance, where they can view and manage all users in
their Figma instance. Figma is hosted on servers in the United States.

Figma is hosted on servers in the United States.

Figma uses sub-processors to provide certain functionality of the Figma platform to our
customers. A full list of our sub-processors and a high level description of their function can be
found here: www.figma.com/sub-processors. Figma uses usage data in a de-identified and
aggregated form to maintain, improve, and enhance Figma's products and services.

Figma will delete your personal data within 30 days of your request. This does not apply to
archival or back-up copies of such data, which will be deleted in accordance with Figma's data
retention schedule. If Figma is required to retain personal data, in archives or otherwise, to
comply with applicable laws it will separate and protect that data from any further data
processing.
